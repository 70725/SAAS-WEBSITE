What are the best practices against cyber-attacks?

Security of Web Development Stages, Keeping Software Updated, Installing SSL, Continuous Vulnerability Scanning and Penetration Testing, Input Sanitization and Validation, Password Security Best Practice, Monitoring Traffic Surges, Content Delivery Networks (CDNs), Implement Web Application Firewall (WAF). For more information, go to the specific tab.

What are the most common cybersecurity attacks?

Malware, Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks, Phishing, Spoofing, Identity-Based Attacks, Code Injection Attacks, Supply Chain Attacks, Insider Threats, DNS Tunneling, and IoT-Based Attacks. For more information, go to the specific tab.

What are the potential consequences of a successful cyber attack on a website?

The economic cost of a cyber attack could be Theft of Corporate Information, Theft of Financial Information, Theft of Money Disruption to Trading, and Loss of Business or Contract. For the reputational damage, this will be a loss of customers, sales, and a reduction in profits/revenue. And lastly for legal consequences, if the data is deliberately or accidentally compromised then there may be fines or regulatory sanctions. For more information, go to the specific tab.

What are some emerging trends in cybersecurity threats and defence mechanisms?

Ransomware Evolution, Supply Chain Vulnerabilities, Multi-Vector Attacks, Hybrid Data Centers, AI-Powered Cyberattacks, Threat Exposure Management, and Security Consolidation. For more information, go to the specific tab.
