    • What are the most common cybersecurity attacks?

    The most common cybersecurity attacks were Malware, Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks, Phishing, Spoofing, Identity-Based Attacks, Code Injection Attacks, Supply Chain Attacks, Insider Threats, DNS Tunneling, and IoT-Based Attacks. These are the most common and simplest ways that a hacker could go into your device and cyber-attack you.

    What is Phishing, Virus, Malware, and Ransomware?

    Phishing is a cyberattack, where malicious actors will send decptive emails and messages to decieve individuals into disclosing personal information. Viruses are programs that replicate themselves, therefore speading quickly to other parts of files and systems, usually used for harmful purposes. Malware is just a term that explains any malicious software that disrupts or gains unauthorized access into computer systems. Ransomware is a malicious system that encyrpts files and computer systems and requesting a ransom for the decryption.

    What is a cyber-attack/cyber-threat?

    Cyber threats and cyber-attacks refers to any possibility of an attempt of malicious intent. This can lead from a range of viruses, ransomware, phishing, and data theft, which is likely the one that most people are afraid of. Although obvious, these can be deadly to individuals, and especially organisations who seem to have lots of employees and workers, therefore increasing the chances of a cyber-attack occurring. This is the reason why understanding how you can protect yourself from these are crucial.

    What is a DDoS attack and how does it work?

    A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack inundates a target server or network with excessive traffic originating from numerous sources, making it inaccessible to genuine users. This is fairly easy to create for people to do, as it only takes two devices in order to start this, but it can have a large impact on peope's computers.
